About Me

General Info:

Initially, I started programming in high school. After joining the robotics team, I brought my team to the world championship for robotics where we would end as semi-finalist. It was from this experience I knew I wanted to go into technology.

In August 2018, I started attending Virginia Tech for a Bachelors of Science in Computer Science. I got involved in a number of hackathons, starting with the Galileo Hackathon. I ended up working on a Virtual Reality Campus Tour. This ended up taking 1st place at the hackathon. This made me further want to get involved in hackathons. From my involvement, I became very interested in Machine Learning, and decided to get a concentration in Data Centric Computing.

I would end up taking 3rd at my next hackathon, SheHacks, in 2020, when I developed an American Sign Language Translator. One of my friends mentioned that they had someone who had difficulty getting their food at restaurants such as McDonalds due to the limited ability to communicate through Sign Language. In order to deal with this problem, my group developed an application in order to attempt to translate the Sign Language.

Finally, I ended up seeing the Bank of America coding challenge. I ended up winning this competition, and I was invited to join the Data and AI team for the Bank after my sophomore year. I worked at Bank of America for the summer after my sophomore year and junior year of college. I am currently going into my 4th year now.

Interests:

When I am not busy coding, I love to ski, hike, and play tennis in my free time. While at Virginia Tech, I would hike at Cascade Falls, Bald Knob which is shown on the left, and Dragons Tooth.
